FreePBX is one of the most trusted PBX systems in the world 📞 It handles extensions, IVRs, queues, and SIP routing extremely well.

But as soon as teams try to run autodialing, CRM workflows, and reporting directly inside FreePBX, things start to get messy.

This article explains how QHT integrates with FreePBX without breaking its core purpose — keeping telephony clean while enabling modern contact center operations.

What FreePBX Does Best

FreePBX shines when it focuses on what it was designed for:

  • Managing extensions and SIP devices
  • Building IVRs and call flows
  • Handling queues and ring strategies
  • Routing calls reliably at scale

In short, FreePBX is an excellent telephony engine.

Where FreePBX Starts to Struggle

Problems appear when teams expect FreePBX to also behave like:

  • An autodialer
  • A CRM workflow engine
  • A reporting and analytics platform
  • An agent productivity system

These responsibilities introduce complexity that does not belong inside a PBX ⚠️

The Clean Integration Principle

QHT follows a simple rule:

Never push business logic into FreePBX.

Instead:

  • FreePBX continues to manage IVRs, queues, and extensions
  • QHT connects via AMI
  • Dialing logic, campaigns, and workflows stay outside

Why This Separation Matters

Keeping FreePBX clean delivers real operational benefits:

  • PBX upgrades without fear
  • Stable IVRs and queues
  • No hidden dialer hacks inside PBX
  • Clear ownership of responsibilities

Key Takeaway

FreePBX should stay focused on telephony. Autodialing, CRM workflows, and reporting belong elsewhere.

QHT bridges that gap cleanly — giving teams power without complexity ⚡
